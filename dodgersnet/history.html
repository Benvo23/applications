World Series Championships

2025
9th World Series Title
Back-to-back champions! The Dodgers repeated as World Series champions, cementing their dynasty status. Now seeking an elusive three-peat in 2026.
2024
8th World Series Title
Defeated the New York Yankees 4-1. Freddie Freeman hit walk-off grand slam in Game 1. Shohei Ohtani's first championship.
2020
7th World Series Title
Defeated the Tampa Bay Rays 4-2 in Arlington, TX. First title since 1988. Corey Seager named World Series MVP.
1988
6th World Series Title
Defeated the Oakland Athletics 4-1. Kirk Gibson's legendary walk-off home run in Game 1. Orel Hershiser named MVP.
1981
5th World Series Title
Defeated the New York Yankees 4-2. "Fernandomania" year with rookie sensation Fernando Valenzuela.
1965
4th World Series Title
Defeated the Minnesota Twins 4-3. Sandy Koufax threw a complete game shutout in Game 7.
1963
3rd World Series Title
Swept the New York Yankees 4-0. Sandy Koufax struck out 15 in Game 1.
1959
2nd World Series Title (1st in LA)
Defeated the Chicago White Sox 4-2. First championship after moving to Los Angeles.
1955
1st World Series Title (Brooklyn)
Defeated the New York Yankees 4-3. "Next Year" finally arrived for Brooklyn. Johnny Podres named MVP.

Franchise Timeline

1883
Team founded as the Brooklyn Atlantics in the Interstate Association.
1890
Team becomes known as the "Bridegrooms" and later the "Trolley Dodgers" (due to Brooklyn's many trolley cars).
1913
Ebbets Field opens as the home of the Brooklyn Dodgers.
1947
Jackie Robinson breaks the color barrier, becoming the first African American to play in MLB in the modern era.
1958
The Dodgers move from Brooklyn to Los Angeles, beginning a new era on the West Coast.
1962
Dodger Stadium opens in Chavez Ravine, becoming the team's iconic home.
2012
Guggenheim Baseball Management purchases the Dodgers for $2.15 billion, a record for any sports team at the time.
2024
Dodgers sign Shohei Ohtani to historic $700M contract and win their 8th World Series title.
